Lesson 6: Non-opioid Alternatives for Pain Management

Nearly everyone has dealt with some level of pain at some time or another.Opioids are often prescribed to treat pain. Opioids can be good at treating acute (sudden and short-term) pain. But opioids arealso highly addictiveand can be dangerous.Opioids were never meantto be used for long-term pain relief. They work best when they are used for a short timeand only to treat severe pain.There are many other treatments available that are may be justas effective as opioids for relieving and managing pain.But everyone responds differently and certain alternatives only work well for specific types of pain. You shouldtalk to your providerto see if any of these options might work for you.

Alternatives to Opioids

Over-the-Counter Medicines:

Ibuprofen(for example, Motrin), acetaminophen(Tylenol),and aspirinare drugs that you can buy without a prescription (that is, over-the-counter) to treat pain. These drugs are often all that is needed to treat many types of pain.

Physical Therapy:

Physical therapy uses special exercises to help you regain motion after an illness or injury. Physical therapy is very good at relieving pain caused by muscle cramping. By improving your natural motion, physical therapy can help reduce pain.

Injections:

Injections with medicines can help you manage pain from nerve damage or muscle spasms.

Massage:

Massage can help train you to relax and reduces muscle tension, which can help improve pain

Meditation:

Meditation and controlled breathing can help you relax, which helps reduce pain.

Therapy:

Psychologists and pain therapist can help you reduce stress. By changing the way you think about pain and teaching you to relax, you can reduce your pain.

Acupuncture:

Acupuncture providespain relief in some patients by interrupting pain signals to the brain.

Surgery:

If other pain management alternatives have not worked, surgery may be a safe, effective way to correct pain caused by injuries in your joint or muscles.Surgery doesn’t always workto treat pain. Surgery should generally be the treatment of last resort for pain.

Using Technology for Pain Management

There are advances in technologyadvancements that can also helpease the pain. In some cases, these methods can provide permanent relief from pain:

Radio frequency Ablation:

Radio frequency ablation, or RFAuses radio waves to burn the specific area where the nerve is that is causing pain. The electricityis transmittedby a needle that’s inserted into the specific nerve. Radiofrequency ablation can provide relief for up to a year.

Nerve Blocks:

Nerve blocks can be injected into your body using X-rays to guide the process. These blocks work by injecting numbing medicineinto the specific area where the nerve is that is causing pain. Nerve blocks can eliminate or dampen chronic pain. In some cases, it can even prevent painfrom developing.

Transcutaneous Nerve Stimulation:

This treatment provides short-term pain relief. Itworks especially well for those experiencing muscle spasms and aches. It works by sending low voltage electric signals to the pained area throughpads placed on theskin.

Pain Pumps:

Certain kinds of pumps can be implanted to give a patient the ability touse a remote control to deliver pain medicineto a specificarea. This type of treatment also provides psychological benefits becauseit gives the patient direct control over their pain.

Lesson 6 : Quiz Questions!

1. True or False: There are new technologies that can help provide pain relief.

2. True or False: Alternative therapies work for everyone.

3. True or False: Surgery isthe best way to treat back pain.

4. True or False: Massage can be an effective way to deal with muscle pain.

5. True or False: Therapy cannot help with pain

Quiz Answers

Question 1: True - There are several technologies that use electricity to provide pain relief to a specific location on the body.

Question 2: False - Some alternative therapies only work for certain types of pain. Everyone also responds differently to each type of therapy. You should talk to your providerbefore you try any alternatives to see if they might work for you.

Question 3: False - Back pain may respond to many other types of treatments. Exercise is often the best treatment. Surgery should usually be seen as a last resort.

Question 4: True - massage often works well to reduce muscle tension, which can reduce pain.

Question 5: False - Because pain and emotions are both processed in the brain, therapy can help treat pain. It can help you relax, which reduces your feelings of pain. It also helps break patterns of negative thinking that may result from chronic pain.
