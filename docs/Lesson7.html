Lesson 7 : Safe Opioid Use

People who take opioids are at risk for opioid dependence,addiction, and overdose. Dependence is when you start to experience withdrawal symptoms when you stop taking a drug. Addiction is when a person becomes so preoccupied with getting a drug that it starts to interfere with their daily life. Overdose is a life-threatening situation that can happen when you take too much of a drug.The risks for these problems increase when opioids are misused. Misuse means you are not taking the medicines the wayyour providertold you to, you are using them to get high, or you are taking someone else's opioids.

You may not need to take opioid medicines to treat your pain. There may also be things your provider needs to know before they prescribe you an opioid medicine. It is important that you have an open conversation with your provide before you start taking an opioid medicine. Here are some of the things you should discusswith your provider:

- Whether there are alternativesthat might treat your pain
- The risks and benefits of taking opioids
- Your medical history and if you or anyone in your family has a history of substance misuse or addiction to drugs or alcohol
- Any other medicines and supplements you are taking
- How much alcohol you drink
- If you are pregnant or planning to become pregnant

If you and your provider decide that you need to take opioids, be sure you understand how to take them safely. You should be sure you talk to your provider so that you understand:

- How to take the medicine
- how much and how often
- How long you will need to take the medicine
- What the possible side effects are
- How you should stop the medicines when you no longer need them. If you have been taking opioids for a while, it can be dangerous to just stop suddenly. You may need to get off the medicines slowly.

What the warning signs of addiction are, so you can watch for them. They includethe following:

- Regularly taking more medicine than you are supposed tooTaking someone else's opioids
- Taking someone else's opioids
- Taking the medicine to get high
- Mood swings, depression, and/or anxiety
- Needing too much or too littlesleep
- Trouble making decisions
- Feeling high or sedated

You should always be careful when taking any medicine. To be sure you take your opioid safely, follow these recommendations:

- Take your medicine exactly as prescribed -do not take extra doses. If your pain is not responding to the amount of medicineprescribed, talk to your provider.
- Check the instructions every time you take a dose
- Do not break, chew, crush, or dissolve opioid pills
- Do not drive or use any machinery that may injure you, especially when you first start the medicine
- Contact your provider if you have side effects
- If you can, use the same pharmacy for all your medicines. The pharmacy's computer system will alert the pharmacist if you are taking two or more medicines that could cause a dangerous interaction.

Lesson 7 : Quiz Questions!

1. True or False: Dependence is the same thing as addiction.

2. True or False: One type of misuse of opioids is when you use someone else’s opioid medicines.

3. True or False: If your pain is worse on a particular day, it’s okay to take extra pills.

4. True or False: Mood swings can be a sign of opioid addition.

5. True or False: It’s a good idea to get all of your prescriptions from the same pharmacy.

Quiz Answers

Question 1: False - Dependence is when you start to experience withdrawal symptoms when you stop taking a drug. Addiction is when a person becomes so preoccupied with getting a drug that it starts to interfere with their daily life.

Question 2: True - Misuse means you are not taking the medicines the way your provider told you to, you are using them to get high, or you are taking someone else's opioids.

Question 3: False - You should always take all of your medicines exactly as prescribed. This is especially true of opioid medicines. You should never take extra doses. If your pain is not responding to the amount of medicineprescribed, you should talk to your provider.

Question 4: True - Addiction to opioids can lead to mood swings, depression, and/or anxiety.

Question 5: True - The pharmacy's computer system will alert the pharmacist if you are taking two or more medicines that could cause a dangerous interaction.
