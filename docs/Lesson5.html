Lesson 5 : Drugs that Should Never be Mixed with Opioids

The number of opioid overdose deaths has increased since the start of the COVID-19 pandemic. In 2021, more than 75,000 people had drug overdoses related to opioid medicines. The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) estimates that prescription opioid medicines are involved in at least 40% of opioid overdose deaths.Taking opioids by themselves can be dangerous if they are taken in large doses.Taking other drugs that affect pain receptorscan increasethe effects of opioid drugs, even if you don’t realize it. This added effect can make an overdose even more likely.It’s important to know about the ways that drugs caninteract.

Opioids have a particularly dangerous reaction with prescription drugs called benzodiazepines, or “benzos” for short. Benzos are used to treat anxiety and insomnia. Examples of benzos include alprazolam, clonazepam, diazepam, clobazam, lorazepam, or chlordiazepoxide. Both opioids and benzos can make you feel sleepy and can slow your breathing. Because they have these similar effects, taking opioids and benzos together is especially dangerous. It is like taking a double dose of medicineand you are more likely to overdose or die.

Alcohol is another drug that's dangerous to mix with opioids. At high levels, alcohol is a depressant. That means it also acts to slow down your breathing and can make you sleepy. Drinking alcohol when you are taking opioids or benzos also increases the risk of an overdose or death.Other types of drugs that can act as depressants, include muscle relaxants, antipsychotic drugs, sleep medicines, and even antihistamines (drugs like Benadryl, that treat allergies).

Drugs that have serotonin can also be dangerous to take with opioids. Some example of these drugs are antidepressants (e.g. fluoxetine, sertraline, and citalopram), drugs to treat migraine headaches (e.g. sumatriptan), and St. John's wort. When you take these drugs with opioids, serotonin can build up in your body. When serotonin builds up to dangerous levels, it is called serotonin syndrome. This build up can cause very high blood pressure and can increase your heart rate. There is a risk of death from serotonin syndrome.

There may be good reasons why you need to take these groups of medicines together. But you should only take the drugs your providerprescribes and in the doses that they prescribe for you.

Lesson 5 : Quiz Questions!

1. True or False:Mixing opioid medicines with other drugs can increase the risk of an overdose.

2. True or False:You should avoid drinking alcohol when you are taking opioid medicines.

3. True or False:It is safe to take herbs and over-the-counter drugs when you are taking opioids.

4. True or False:You should be sure your providerknows all the medicines you are taking.

5. True or False:It is safe to take antidepressants when you take opioid medicines.

Quiz Answers

Question 1: True: There are other types of drugs that have effects similar to opioid medicines. Taking these groups of drugs together can increase feelings of sleepiness and can slow your heart rate. A person can die from this type of drug overdose.

Question 2: True: Alcohol is a drug. At higher levels of alcohol intake, you may start to feel sleepy. Drinking alcohol when you are taking an opioid medicinecan increase the risk of an overdose.

Question 3: False: Some herbs, like St. John’s wort, can interact with opioids. You should be sure to tell your providerabout all the drugs, supplements, and herbs that you use.

Question 4: True: There may be good reasons for you to take drugs that may interact with opioids. Your providercan decide if it is safe to take the medicines together.

Question 5: False:Some types of antidepressants can interact with opioids. Antidepressants, and other drugs, that have serotonin can cause a build-up of serotonin in your body. This build-up can cause very high blood pressure and heart rate. There is a risk of death with the build-up of serotonin in your body.
