Lesson 2: Talking About Pain With Providers

Pain is a very personal experience that varies from person to person. What may feel painful to you may not be painful to someone else. Learning how to talk about your pain can make it easier to find the right ways to treat it. It helps to keep track of your pain in the form of a diary. You can use the acronym “OLDCARTS” to help you remember what to keep track of. “OLDCARTS” stands for:

Onset: when the pain started

Location - where you feel the pain

Duration - how long you have had the pain, how long the pain lasts,or how often pain occurs

Characteristics - how the pain feels

Relieving factors - what, if anything, makes the pain better

Treatments - what medicines or treatments you have used for the pain

Severity - how severe the pain is on a scale of 0 (no pain) to 10 (the worst pain you have ever felt)
