Lesson 8 : Safe Opioid Storage

It is very important to store opioids safely. Improper use of pain medicine is a leading cause of accidental death. Safe storage can prevent misuse of opioids and accidental overdoses.

There are several things to remember to safely store opioids:

Store opioids in a locked container.

This could be a medicine cabinet with a lock or a lockbox. Opioids left in an unlocked space could be misused or taken to be sold.

Keep opioids in their original package.

Many pills look similar to each other. Keeping medicines in their original containers prevents accidental misuse.

Keep opioids out of children's reach.

Prain relievers are a leading cause of serious poisoning of children and pets when they are left where others can get them. Children can overdose on dosages that are safe for adults. It is important to teach children about safety in use of prescription medicines. Never tell children that medicine is "candy" as a way to encourage them to take it. Be sure children understand that they should only take medicines under adult supervision.

Never sell or share your medicines.

Your prescription is for you. Your health care provider considers many factors when prescribing opioids. What's safe for you might lead to an overdose for someone else.

If someone steals your opioid medicines or prescription, report the theft to the police.

You may be responsible for an accidental overdose. Reporting any theft immediately to the police could save a life and will protect you from prosecution.

Lesson 8 : Quiz Questions!

1. True or False: It is enough to store opioids on a high shelf to keep them out of children’s reach.

2. True or False: Keeping track of the number of pills is important for safe opioid storage.

3. True or False: If someone has stolen your medicines, you should report it to the police.

4. True or False: It is okay to share your opioids with others.

5. True or False: Putting opioids in an unlabeled container is a good way to “hide” them from others.

Quiz Answers

Question 1: False - Whenever possible, opioids should be stored in a locked container to be sure that children cannot get to them.

Question 2: True - Keeping track of the number of pills you have taken helps to ensure that no pills have gone missing

Question 3: True - You may be responsible for an accidental overdose if someone misuses your prescription. Reporting any theft immediately to the police could save a life and will protect you from prosecution.

Question 4: False - Every prescription medicine should only be used by the person for whom it is prescribed. This is especially true for opioids due to the risk of addiction and overdose.

Question 5: False - Because so many pills can look similar, you should always store medicines in their original containers. This helps to prevent accidental misuse.
